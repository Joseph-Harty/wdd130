Client & Developer Final Project Report

Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

Since this is an ROTC recruiting page, I decided on the content and images by focusing on what would best encourage and inspire the reader to join the program. I specifically chose images and content from the school and current cadets to keep it authentic and motivating.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

Our communication was mostly in person and through email. It worked out perfectly, and there were no issues at all.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

Yes, absolutely. The developer did a great job with everything and fully met my expectations for the site.

What will you do differently next time you hire a developer to do a project for you?

Since this went so smoothly, I wouldn't change much. I would just ensure we maintain the same great in-person and email communication from the start.

Lead Developer Report

How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?

I communicated with my client mostly in person and via email. It was highly effective, easy to get feedback on the site layout, and there were no communication issues.

How did you handle publishing your clients site and sharing it with the client?

I published the site using GitHub Pages. Once the files were committed and pushed to the main branch, I enabled GitHub Pages and shared the live URL with the client for their review.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?

Managing the site with GitHub was a great learning experience. It helped keep track of my changes as I built out the content, and it provided a reliable way to host the final project.

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

The most challenging part was getting the page layouts to look exactly right on smaller screens. I was able to overcome this by reviewing media queries and adjusting the styling for the images and text blocks.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

I really enjoyed the collaborative process and seeing the final website come together. Since I was the developer for the Murchison site, the most rewarding part of this project was being able to take my client's ideas, build a fully functional website from scratch, and deliver a final product that made them happy.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

If I could do it again, I would spend a bit more time mapping out the exact HTML structure before jumping into the code to make the styling phase even faster.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

Yes, I feel much more confident. Building a site with a specific purpose and target audience, managing it with GitHub, and working through the client-developer dynamic makes me feel ready to tackle real-world projects.
